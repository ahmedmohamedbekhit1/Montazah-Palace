History

Montaza Palace was built in 1892 by King Abbas II as a hunting lodge and summer retreat. It is located in the Montaza district of Alexandria, Egypt, overlooking the Mediterranean Sea.

The palace is surrounded by a large park, which was originally designed as a private garden for the royal family. Today, the park is open to the public and features a variety of gardens, fountains, and walking paths.

Architecture

Montaza Palace is a blend of Islamic, Ottoman, and European architectural styles. The palace is made up of several buildings, including the main palace, a mosque, a clock tower, and several guest houses.

The main palace features a large dome and several smaller domes, as well as ornate facades and decorative elements. The interior of the palace is equally impressive, with marble columns, intricate mosaics, and gilded ceilings.
